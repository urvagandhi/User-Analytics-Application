Diving Guides & Resources

10 Tips for Your First Open Water Dive

Your first dive can be intimidating. Remember to always breathe continuously, equalize early and often, and trust your instructor. Equipment checks are essential before every dive to ensure your BCD, regulator, and weights are properly configured.

Choosing the Right Wetsuit Thickness

Water temperature dictates your thermal protection. For tropical waters (75°F+), a 3mm shorty or full suit is usually sufficient. For temperate waters (60-75°F), consider a 5mm or 7mm suit. Always err on the side of warmth, as water conducts heat away from your body 25 times faster than air.

Top 5 Dive Destinations for 2026

From the vibrant coral gardens of Raja Ampat to the haunting shipwrecks of Chuuk Lagoon, explore the most breathtaking underwater environments. Advanced booking is highly recommended as liveaboards fill up months in advance.

Understanding Nitrox

Enriched Air Nitrox (EANx) contains a higher percentage of oxygen and lower percentage of nitrogen than regular air. This allows for longer bottom times and shorter surface intervals, but comes with stricter depth limits due to oxygen toxicity risks.
